Strategy

Cadle, Paul and Turner say the following Business Analysis techniques are among the most useful:

CATWOE: Customer(s), Actor(s), Transformation, Weltanschauung, Owner, Environment
Often organisational problems are caused by differences of Weltanschauung between stakeholders. Unless the differences are brought out and discussed then they undermine efforts towards new processes and systems.

Business process modelling (swimlanes)
Models reveal inconsistencies in a process and bring out the "as is" status quo.

Use cases
Rough pictures of what a new system will look like, and what it will do.

Workshops
Properly facilitated, they are fast and build consensus and support for the way forward.

Mind maps
Summarise the essence of a business issue, or the essence of discussion at a meeting.

Interviewing
A soft skill that may reveal issues that don't come out of a workshop.

Organisation diagram
Shows the main stakeholders, key competitors and customers, before getting into the detail.

Scenario analysis
Sometimes enables users to articulate requirements.

Cost benefit analysis
Identify tangible and intangible cases for business analysis work.

SWOT
BA work is all about exploiting strengths, overcoming weaknesses, seizing opportunities and staving off threats.

Prioritisation
Nobody can do everything at once, and management is often a matter of difficult choices between alternatives. Try MoSCoW.
